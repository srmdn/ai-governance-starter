# Business AI Policy Baseline

Use this policy when AI is involved in customer-facing product, pricing, or GTM decisions.

## Scope

Applies to:
- AI-assisted product strategy and roadmap decisions
- AI-generated customer-facing content (marketing, sales, support)
- AI-assisted pricing and packaging analysis
- AI workflows involving customer or commercial data

## Core Principle

AI can accelerate analysis and drafting, but final business decisions remain human-owned and evidence-backed.

## Required Controls

1. All market or competitor claims must be source-backed before publication.
2. Pricing recommendations require explicit assumptions and sensitivity analysis.
3. Customer-facing copy generated by AI requires human legal/compliance review.
4. Material strategy decisions must be documented in a decision record.
5. Sensitive customer/commercial data must follow approved data handling rules.

## Prohibited Practices

- Publishing AI-generated factual claims without verification.
- Using AI to fabricate testimonials, case studies, or customer quotes.
- Misrepresenting AI outputs as validated market evidence.
- Delegating final pricing or legal decisions to AI tools.

## Minimum Evidence Standard

For strategy docs, include:
- problem statement
- target segment definition
- alternatives considered
- assumptions and unknowns
- success metrics and kill criteria

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to maintainers when:
- a business decision affects legal/compliance posture
- a model/provider change impacts customer data handling
- a GTM claim cannot be independently verified
